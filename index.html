About Me

Sean has been a full-time staff member at the TTU Library Makerspace since 2017. Beyond keeping the 3D printers running and managing the student workers, he has enjoyed developing new Makerspace workshops every year. Sean has taught on topics ranging from Circuit-Bending, TinkerCAD, Rhinoceros3D, Blender, Laser-cutting, Solidworks and Autodesk’s Inventor to coding in Scratch, MakeCode, Python and MATLAB. Sean spent many years enthusiastically mentoring students and fellow mentors through Texas Tech’s Mentor Tech program and as an advisor for the Makerspace Student Organization.

Sean acquired his BFA in Jewelry/Metal Design from Texas Tech University and his MFA in Jewelry/Metalsmithing/Enameling from Kent State University. From 2010-2022, Sean served as an instructor at South Plains College teaching online Art Appreciation classes. Sean is currently an artist-in-residence in a Work Studio at the Charles Adams Studio Project (CASP) where he shows his jewelry and art installations regularly on the Lubbock First Friday Art Trail. He also enjoys playing guitar, taking electrical engineering classes, knitting, and conducting mad science.
